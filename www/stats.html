DKIM Statistics

As of v2.1.0, OpenDKIM has a comprehensive statistics gathering package included as an option. If enabled, it will log to a database information about the DKIM properties of every message that the filter sees, including those that weren't even signed.

The purpose of this statistics gathering is twofold:

  1. To gather statistics you can use to analyze what you're seeing at your site in terms of signed messages, unsigned messages, messages that fail verification, messages that fail ADSP policy checks, multiply-signed messages, third-party signatures, etc.
  2. To submit the above statistics, with most of the identifiable properties hidden if you wish, back to The OpenDKIM Project so we can measure DKIM's deployment, level of activity and general effectiveness, and possibly identify specific problem areas.

You are encouraged to participate in the latter. This is being done in conjunction with the Internet Engineering Task Force's DKIM Working Group, the body that created the DKIM standard. We will submit the names of any company or person that contributes data as part of our report to that body for appropriate credit.

You are not required to participate. In fact, it is necessary to specify a specific build-time option just to get the statistics stuff compiled in (and the filter works just fine without doing so), and then you'd need to take specific additional actions to submit the data back to The OpenDKIM Project. The data submitted will identify the submitter, but you can choose to submit only hashes of the author domain of those messages; this way we can't tell what the author domain was, but we can tell if two different messages about which you submit data are from the same author domain. This is important for accurate data collection.

The submission mechanism on our side has not yet been installed and configured. This page will be updated, and an announcement sent out, once that mechanism is up and working.

Please submit any questions about this effort to the opendkim-users mailing list.
